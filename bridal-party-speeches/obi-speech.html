Obi

Growing up as the youngest of two with my dad living abroad for the most of my teenage life. I was blessed to have an older sister, Amaka there to help guide my footsteps and advise me on some of the unspoken day-to-day issues concerning 'becoming a man'. However, as you can imagine, this wasn't always the most straight forward of roles and being my elder cousin, this is where Ejay stepped in.

Being of similar age to my sister, Amaka and Ejay would keep in contact about my well being, with Ejay advising both her and consequently myself, on how best to keep me on the straight and narrow. Although I was too young to really understand or appreciate the interest being taken in me, I was always aware of it.

I remember being around 13 or so and spending a weekend with Ejay. He (reluctantly) gave me permission to get a haircut both of our parents didn't exactly approve of - I think Nike ticks where in fashion back then lol. He then bought me my first ever Morley's and I remember ending the weekend playing basketball with him and the boys. I left his house wanting to be just like him. A few years later, Ejay invited me to come and spend a weekend with him in Nottingham where I was first introduced to Swodei. She greeted me with open arms and a warm smile that made me feel nothing but welcome. Now, it brings me nothing but joy to be able to do the same in welcoming her to the family and embracing her as both a cousin and a sister.

I wish the two of you eternal joy and happiness and I pray that God continues to guide and bless your union.
